## HEALTHCARE SYSTEM MECHANICS

1. **Hospital Chargemaster Price Opacity** — Hospitals maintain a hidden master price list (chargemaster) that bears no relationship to actual negotiated rates, creating a structural information asymmetry that inflates billing and makes cost comparison impossible for patients.

2. **Prior Authorization Delay Loop** — Insurance prior authorization requirements introduce mandatory procedural latency between physician diagnosis and treatment, measurably delaying care and adding administrative cost with no clinical decision-making value.

3. **Certificate of Need Regulatory Capture** — State CON laws require government approval before new healthcare facilities can open, structurally suppressing supply competition while protecting incumbent hospital systems that sit on the approval boards.

4. **Employer-Tied Health Insurance Lock-In** — Linking health insurance to employment status creates structural job lock, measurably reducing worker mobility and labor market fluidity by binding coverage to a single employer relationship.

5. **Fee-for-Service Volume Incentive** — Medicare and private insurer reimbursement structures pay per procedure rather than per outcome, creating a financial mechanism that rewards more interventions regardless of patient benefit.

6. **Pharmacy Benefit Manager Spread Pricing** — PBMs collect the difference between what insurers pay for drugs and what pharmacies receive, a structural arbitrage layer that extracts margin without adding clinical value and obscures true drug costs.

7. **340B Drug Pricing Program Arbitrage** — The 340B program grants hospitals discounts on drugs intended for low-income patients but does not require those savings be passed to patients, creating a structural revenue extraction mechanism.

## HOUSING / ZONING / LAND USE

8. **Single-Family Zoning Supply Suppression** — Municipal zoning codes that mandate single-family-only land use mathematically constrain housing unit density, creating a structural supply ceiling that transmits directly into price floors.

9. **Inclusionary Zoning Cost Pass-Through** — Mandatory affordable unit set-asides in market-rate developments shift subsidy costs to market-rate buyers, structurally increasing the price of non-subsidized units and producing a net housing affordability paradox.

10. **Parking Minimum Mandate Land Waste** — Local codes requiring minimum parking spaces per unit consume buildable land and increase construction costs, structurally reducing unit yield per acre regardless of actual parking demand.

11. **Environmental Review Weaponization** — CEQA and similar state environmental review laws can be invoked by any party to block housing construction, creating a procedural veto mechanism that incumbents use to suppress competing supply.

12. **Property Tax Assessment Lag** — Assessment cycles that update valuations infrequently create structural windfall subsidies for long-term owners, distorting holding incentives and reducing housing market turnover.

13. **Homeowner Association Governance Capture** — HOA covenants create private governance structures with enforcement power but no democratic accountability, allowing small boards to impose structural barriers on land use with no public oversight mechanism.

## CRIMINAL JUSTICE / POLICING / PRISONS

14. **Cash Bail Wealth Sorting Mechanism** — Pretrial release conditioned on cash payment structurally sorts defendants by net worth rather than flight risk or public safety, producing measurably different case outcomes for identical charges based solely on financial status.

15. **Prosecutorial Charging Discretion Opacity** — Prosecutors hold unreviewed discretion to select charge severity, creating a structural leverage asymmetry that coerces plea agreements regardless of trial merit.

16. **Civil Asset Forfeiture Revenue Dependency** — Departments that retain forfeiture proceeds develop structural revenue dependency on seizure activity, creating a financial incentive mechanism decoupled from criminal conviction.

17. **Prison Labor Wage Suppression** — Correctional facilities deploy incarcerated labor at wages below minimum wage floors, structurally undercutting external labor markets in industries where prison-made goods compete.

18. **Qualified Immunity Accountability Firewall** — The qualified immunity doctrine requires plaintiffs to identify a prior case with identical facts before civil rights claims proceed, creating a structural precedent bootstrapping problem that blocks novel violations from ever generating accountability.

19. **Police Union Contract Accountability Erasure** — Collective bargaining agreements for law enforcement routinely include provisions that expunge disciplinary records and restrict complaint investigation timelines, structurally dismantling civilian oversight mechanisms after the fact.

20. **Mandatory Minimum Sentencing Judicial Bypass** — Legislatively fixed minimum sentences remove judicial discretion at sentencing, transferring sentencing power from judges to prosecutors at the charging stage where it is exercised without a public record.

## IMMIGRATION SYSTEM STRUCTURE

21. **Immigration Court Backlog Limbo Mechanism** — A structurally underfunded immigration court docket creates multi-year case backlogs, producing a de facto limbo status that is neither lawful residence nor deportation and persists independently of case merit.

22. **Visa Retrogression Structural Freeze** — Per-country caps on employment-based immigrant visas create decades-long queues for applicants from high-demand countries, structurally freezing workers in temporary visa status with no path to permanence.

23. **Asylum Credible Fear Interview Inconsistency** — Asylum grant rates vary by more than 50 percentage points across individual immigration judges hearing identical fact patterns, revealing a structural variance in adjudication that cannot be explained by case merit alone.

24. **Detention Bed Quota Revenue Mechanism** — Congressional mandates and ICE contracts that require a minimum number of detention beds create structural financial incentives to maintain detention populations regardless of enforcement need.

## TAX CODE MECHANICS

25. **Carried Interest Capital Gains Reclassification** — The carried interest provision allows fund managers to classify ordinary labor income as capital gains, structurally reducing their effective tax rate relative to workers with equivalent income earned as wages.

26. **Step-Up Basis Inheritance Wealth Lock** — Assets transferred at death receive a cost basis reset to current market value, structurally exempting unrealized lifetime gains from income tax and concentrating inherited capital advantage.

27. **Pass-Through Entity Deduction Complexity Moat** — Section 199A pass-through deductions require professional interpretation to optimize, creating a structural compliance cost differential that disproportionately benefits entities with tax counsel over those without.

28. **Opportunity Zone Self-Certification Capture** — Opportunity Zone designations relied on governors' self-reported nominations, with no structural requirement to select distressed areas, allowing designation of already-appreciating real estate markets for tax deferral benefits.

29. **Corporate Offshore Profit Shifting** — Transfer pricing rules allow multinational corporations to assign profits to low-tax jurisdictions through intracompany transactions at non-market rates, structurally reducing domestic taxable income without operational relocation.

## MILITARY-INDUSTRIAL COMPLEX

30. **Cost-Plus Defense Contract Incentive Inversion** — Cost-plus contracting structures reimburse contractors for all costs plus a guaranteed profit margin, removing structural incentives for efficiency and rewarding cost growth over performance.

31. **Congressional Defense Earmark Geographic Distribution** — Defense contracts and bases are geographically distributed across congressional districts in a pattern that structurally converts military spending into incumbent protection, making base closures politically impossible regardless of strategic necessity.

32. **Revolving Door Regulatory Capture in Defense Procurement** — The measurable flow of DoD acquisition officers into defense contractor employment creates a structural conflict-of-interest mechanism during the procurement period preceding departure.

33. **Weapons System Sunk Cost Continuation** — Major weapons programs accumulate contractor workforce and supply chain dependencies early in development, creating a structural political cost to cancellation that exceeds the financial cost of continuing failed programs.

## PHARMACEUTICAL INDUSTRY

34. **Patent Evergreening Exclusivity Extension** — Pharmaceutical manufacturers file secondary patents on minor reformulations of existing drugs to extend market exclusivity beyond primary patent expiration, structurally delaying generic competition without therapeutic innovation.

35. **FDA Orphan Drug Designation Abuse** — The orphan drug program grants extended exclusivity for drugs targeting small patient populations but has been applied to drugs with large markets by defining populations narrowly, structurally granting monopoly pricing for common conditions.

36. **Pharmacy Benefit Manager Formulary Kickback** — Rebates paid by pharmaceutical manufacturers to PBMs for formulary placement are not passed to patients at point of sale, creating a structural pricing mechanism that favors high-list-price drugs with large rebate margins over lower-cost alternatives.

37. **Clinical Trial Publication Bias** — Pharmaceutical companies control trial publication, creating a structural asymmetry where negative trial results are suppressed and positive results are published, distorting physician prescribing information at the evidence base level.

## TECH MONOPOLY / PLATFORM GOVERNANCE

38. **App Store Gatekeeper Tax** — Apple and Google's mandatory 15–30% commission on all in-app transactions in their controlled distribution monopolies functions as a structural platform tax with no competitive alternative for mobile software distribution.

39. **Algorithmic Amplification Opacity** — Social media ranking algorithms are proprietary and unaudited, creating a structural information asymmetry where platform operators can shape public discourse through amplification choices that are neither visible nor accountable to users or regulators.

40. **Consent Fatigue Dark Pattern Mechanism** — Cookie consent and privacy opt-out interfaces are structurally designed to maximize consent through complexity and friction, producing consent records that do not reflect actual user preference.

41. **Acquisition-Based Nascent Competitor Elimination** — Platform companies' acquisition of potential competitors before they achieve market scale prevents the formation of structural competition, a mechanism measurable by comparing acquisition frequency to market entry survival rates.

42. **Section 230 Liability Shield Asymmetry** — Platform immunity from content liability under Section 230 applies regardless of the degree to which algorithmic amplification actively promotes harmful content, creating a structural mismatch between editorial control and legal responsibility.

## BANKING / FINANCIAL REGULATION

43. **Too-Big-to-Fail Implicit Subsidy** — Systemically important financial institutions receive an implicit government backstop that lowers their borrowing costs relative to smaller competitors, structurally subsidizing concentration and moral hazard simultaneously.

44. **Overdraft Fee Revenue Dependency** — Bank revenue models built on overdraft fee income structurally target low-balance customers with the highest fees, a cross-subsidization mechanism that extracts the most from the least financially resilient depositors.

45. **Credit Score Thin-File Exclusion Loop** — Credit scoring models require prior credit history to generate scores, structurally preventing credit-invisible populations from establishing creditworthiness through any mechanism other than secured products that require capital they lack.

46. **Ratings Agency Issuer-Pay Conflict** — Credit rating agencies are paid by the issuers whose instruments they rate, creating a structural conflict of interest that produces measurably inflated ratings relative to subsequent default outcomes.

47. **Regulatory Arbitrage Through Charter Shopping** — Financial institutions can select their primary regulator by choosing state or federal charter type, creating structural competition among regulators to offer favorable oversight as an incentive for charter capture.

## ENVIRONMENTAL REGULATION

48. **Pollution Permit Grandfathering Lock-In** — Existing industrial facilities often receive grandfathered emission permits that new entrants cannot obtain, structurally protecting incumbent polluters from competitive pressure and regulatory modernization simultaneously.

49. **Superfund Orphan Site Liability Diffusion** — When responsible parties for contaminated sites are insolvent or dissolved, cleanup costs default to the public via the Superfund trust, creating a structural mechanism that privatizes industrial gain while socializing environmental cost.

50. **Environmental Impact Statement Procedural Weaponization** — EIS requirements can be triggered and extended through litigation by parties whose actual goal is project delay rather than environmental protection, structurally converting a disclosure mechanism into a veto instrument.

51. **Agricultural Runoff Clean Water Act Exemption** — Nonpoint source agricultural pollution is structurally exempted from Clean Water Act permit requirements, creating a regulatory gap that accounts for the largest share of U.S. water quality impairment while remaining unregulated.

## LABOR LAW / UNIONS / WORKER CLASSIFICATION

52. **Independent Contractor Misclassification Arbitrage** — The structural ambiguity in worker classification standards allows employers to capture employee labor while denying employment benefits, shifting payroll tax and benefit costs to the worker and public systems.

53. **National Labor Relations Board Election Delay Mechanism** — The gap between union election petition filing and election date allows employer-initiated procedural delays that measurably reduce union win rates, structurally advantaging employer opposition campaigns.

54. **Right-to-Work Free Rider Problem** — Right-to-work laws allow workers to receive union-negotiated benefits without paying union dues, creating a structural collective action problem that financially weakens unions in states that adopt them.

55. **Non-Compete Agreement Labor Market Freezing** — Non-compete clauses imposed on non-managerial workers structurally reduce labor market competition for those workers' skills, suppressing wage growth through mobility restriction rather than productivity.

56. **Tip Credit Minimum Wage Suppression** — The federal tip credit allows employers to pay tipped workers a sub-minimum base wage and attribute tips toward minimum wage compliance, structurally shifting wage floor responsibility from employer to customer.

## ELECTORAL MECHANICS (BEYOND TWO-PARTY)

57. **Plurality Voting Spoiler Effect** — First-past-the-post voting in multi-candidate races structurally produces spoiler dynamics where a third candidate's presence changes the winner without winning, a mathematically demonstrable mechanism that suppresses voter preference expression.

58. **Gerrymandering Incumbent Protection Feedback** — District boundary drawing by incumbent legislatures structurally entrenches the drawing party's majority, creating a self-reinforcing cycle where electoral outcomes determine the rules that determine future electoral outcomes.

59. **Campaign Finance Disclosure Lag** — Mandatory disclosure filing deadlines that trail election dates by weeks create a structural information gap during the period when voter decisions are being made, reducing disclosure's accountability function to zero at the moment of peak relevance.

60. **Ballot Access Signature Threshold Differential** — Major party candidates face structurally lower ballot access requirements than independent or minor-party candidates in most states, creating an entry barrier that is legally imposed rather than preference-based.

61. **Closed Primary Voter Suppression** — Closed primary systems restrict primary participation to registered party members, structurally concentrating effective electoral power in a subset of the electorate and incentivizing candidates toward ideological extremes where base voters concentrate.

62. **Local TV Station Consolidation Homogenization** — FCC waivers allowing single ownership of multiple local television stations in the same market structurally eliminate locally produced competing news coverage, homogenizing political framing across what appears to be separate outlets.

63. **Newspaper Hedge Fund Acquisition Strip-Mining** — Private equity acquisition of local newspapers followed by cost-cutting and closure creates a structural journalism desert in affected markets, measurably reducing local electoral accountability.

64. **Nielsen Ratings Circular Reference** — Broadcast advertising rates are set by Nielsen ratings, which are measured using a structurally unrepresentative sample panel, creating a pricing mechanism built on a measurement artifact rather than actual audience behavior.

65. **Search Engine Indexing Gatekeeping** — Google's indexing and ranking algorithms determine which information is practically accessible on the web, creating a structural single point of editorial control over public information access that operates with no public accountability mechanism.

## LOCAL GOVERNMENT / CITY COUNCIL MECHANICS

66. **Off-Cycle Election Turnout Suppression** — Scheduling local elections in odd years or non-November dates structurally reduces voter participation, concentrating effective electoral power in organized constituencies (unions, real estate interests) while depressing general public influence.

67. **Tax Increment Financing Diversion** — TIF districts freeze property tax distributions to existing recipients and redirect increment growth to redevelopment, structurally starving school districts and public services in developing areas for decades.

68. **City Council At-Large vs. District Representation Capture** — At-large council structures allow majority blocs to structurally prevent minority-community representation that district systems would produce, a measurable outcome difference with the same underlying voter preference distribution.

69. **Municipal Bond Rating Dependency** — City budget decisions are structurally constrained by bond rating agency assessments, transferring effective fiscal governance from elected officials to unelected private financial analysts with no democratic accountability.

## WATER / INFRASTRUCTURE / PUBLIC WORKS

70. **Water Rights Prior Appropriation Lock** — Western U.S. prior appropriation water law grants senior rights holders structural priority regardless of current use efficiency or social value, preventing market-based reallocation to higher-value uses during scarcity.

71. **Deferred Infrastructure Maintenance Compounding** — Structural incentive misalignment between maintenance cost timing (future) and political benefit timing (present) produces systematically deferred maintenance, with repair costs compounding geometrically while accounting treats them as a future liability.

72. **Combined Sewer Overflow Regulatory Non-Compliance** — Aging combined sewer systems that discharge untreated sewage during rain events are in documented Clean Water Act violation but receive perpetual compliance schedule extensions, creating a structural enforcement exemption for infrastructure legacy costs.

73. **Lead Service Line Replacement Cost Assignment** — When lead service lines extend from the municipal main to the household connection, the homeowner-owned portion requires private financing for replacement, structurally concentrating remediation costs on the low-income households most likely to have legacy plumbing.

## CHILD WELFARE SYSTEM

74. **Foster Care Placement Instability Trauma Compounding** — Funding structures that reimburse per-placement rather than per-stability create a structural financial incentive for placement churning, producing measurable developmental harm through disrupted attachments.

75. **Family Policing Poverty Conflation** — Mandatory reporting and removal standards that treat poverty-related conditions (food insecurity, housing instability) as neglect structurally route economic problems through a child separation system rather than a support system.

76. **Aging-Out Benefit Cliff** — Youth exiting foster care at age 18 lose housing, stipend, and support eligibility simultaneously, creating a structural benefit cliff that produces measurable spikes in homelessness and incarceration at the age-18 transition.

## MENTAL HEALTH SYSTEM

77. **Mental Health Parity Law Non-Enforcement** — The Mental Health Parity and Addiction Equity Act requires equivalent coverage for mental health and medical conditions, but enforcement mechanisms are structurally weak enough that insurers routinely deny mental health claims at measurably higher rates than equivalent medical claims without consequence.

78. **Psychiatric Bed Elimination Criminalization Transfer** — Decades of state psychiatric hospital closures without community-based replacement have structurally transferred the mentally ill population from health facilities to jails and prisons, making correctional facilities the de facto largest mental health providers.

79. **Involuntary Hold 72-Hour Discharge Cliff** — Emergency psychiatric hold laws permit 72-hour holds but provide no structural continuum of care mechanism at discharge, producing a revolving door of crisis presentation without treatment attachment.

## VETERAN AFFAIRS

80. **VA Claims Backlog Adjudication Bottleneck** — Veterans' disability claims processing delays are structurally created by a paper-based, centralized adjudication system that cannot process claim volume, producing benefit access delays measured in years while service-connected conditions worsen.

81. **Military Sexual Trauma Reporting Chain Conflict** — Mandatory reporting of sexual assault through the command chain structurally routes victim reports through the same hierarchy that contains alleged perpetrators, producing measurably lower reporting rates than civilian systems with independent reporting mechanisms.

82. **Discharge Status Benefits Firewall** — Veterans with Other-Than-Honorable discharges are structurally barred from VA benefits regardless of service-connected conditions, a categorical exclusion that disproportionately affects veterans separated for conditions (PTSD, MST) that are themselves service-connected.

## SOCIAL SECURITY / MEDICARE / MEDICAID MECHANICS

83. **Social Security Payroll Tax Wage Cap Regressive Inversion** — The Social Security payroll tax applies only to wages below a threshold (~$168,000 in 2024), structurally making the effective tax rate fall as income rises above the cap — a regressive mechanism within a nominally universal program.

84. **Medicaid Cliff Asset Test** — Medicaid eligibility asset tests create a structural benefit cliff where accumulating modest savings causes immediate full loss of coverage, financially penalizing asset-building behavior in the lowest-income population.

85. **Medicare Advantage Risk Score Upcoding** — Medicare Advantage plans are reimbursed based on patient risk scores they themselves code, creating a structural incentive for diagnosis inflation that overstates patient complexity and extracts excess federal reimbursement.

## AGRICULTURAL SUBSIDIES / FOOD SYSTEM

86. **Commodity Subsidy Concentration Mechanism** — Federal commodity subsidies are distributed per-acre, structurally directing the majority of payments to the largest farm operations and reinforcing agricultural consolidation rather than supporting the small farms the program rhetorically invokes.

87. **SNAP Benefit Calculation Purchasing Power Lag** — SNAP benefit levels are calculated from a Thrifty Food Plan that historically underestimated actual food costs, creating a structural gap between benefit levels and nutritional adequacy that persisted for decades before a 2021 adjustment.

88. **Vertical Integration Processor Monopsony** — In poultry and hog production, four or fewer processors control enough market share in regional supply chains to function as structural monopsonies, suppressing contracted farmer income through take-it-or-leave-it contract terms.

## PATENT / INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

89. **Patent Troll Non-Practicing Entity Litigation Tax** — Non-practicing entities that hold patents solely to extract licensing revenue through litigation asymmetry structurally impose a tax on operating companies, with settlement costs below litigation costs functioning as an extortion mechanism regardless of patent validity.

90. **Copyright Term Retroactive Extension** — Congressional copyright term extensions applied retroactively to existing works prevent public domain expansion without incentivizing new creation, structurally serving incumbent rights-holder revenue rather than the constitutional purpose of promoting progress.

91. **Standard-Essential Patent Hold-Up** — When patents are declared essential to interoperability standards, holders gain structural leverage to demand supra-FRAND licensing terms because the switching cost of standard avoidance exceeds the licensing fee, converting a public standards process into a private tax mechanism.

92. **Third-Party Doctrine Data Exposure** — The legal doctrine holding that data voluntarily shared with third parties (banks, telecoms, apps) loses Fourth Amendment protection creates a structural loophole where the entirety of digital life is accessible to government without a warrant.

93. **Data Broker Regulatory Vacuum** — The absence of a federal data broker registration or opt-out mechanism creates a structural market for personal data that operates entirely outside consumer knowledge or consent, with no accountability mechanism for secondary and tertiary data sales.

94. **Surveillance Technology Procurement Opacity** — Law enforcement acquisition of cell-site simulators, facial recognition, and license plate readers through non-public procurement channels creates a structural accountability gap where surveillance capabilities are deployed in communities with no public deliberation.

## PUBLIC TRANSIT / TRANSPORTATION

97. **Highway-Transit Funding Asymmetry** — Federal transportation funding formulas structurally direct the majority of surface transportation funds to highway capacity expansion while transit receives a minority share, locking in automobile dependency through infrastructure investment ratios regardless of urban population preferences.

98. **Last-Mile Gap Induced Non-Use** — Fixed-route transit systems without structural first/last-mile connection mechanisms produce ridership gaps in origin-destination pairs where only one end has access, creating a mode-choice barrier that is a network design failure rather than a preference failure.

## TELECOM / BROADBAND MONOPOLY

99. **Incumbent ISP Franchise Agreement Market Partition** — Municipal franchise agreements historically granted exclusive or near-exclusive geographic territories to cable and telephone incumbents, structurally preventing competitive broadband entry in most American residential markets and producing the highest prices among peer nations.

100. **Broadband Definition Threshold Manipulation** — The FCC's authority to regulate broadband and report on competition depends on the threshold speed used to define "broadband," creating a structural incentive for the regulator to set definitions that minimize the documented problem size rather than reflect actual consumer utility.
