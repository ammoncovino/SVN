# SVN — BIOME STRUCTURE

## Principle

Like Reddit's subreddit model — don't reinvent this. Different spaces for different scales and topics. But unlike Reddit, the biology rules apply everywhere. Every biome follows the same energy mechanics.

---

## BIOME LAYERS (Scale-Based)

The system organizes by proximity — from your front door to the planet. This maps to real problem-solving at every level.

### Layer 1 — PLOT (Hyperlocal)
**Your street. Your block. Your immediate environment.**

Examples:
- "The power lines in front of my house are ugly. Underground? Cost?"
- "The drainage ditch on 5th street floods every spring"
- "My neighbor's fence is falling into the sidewalk"

Properties:
- Geography-locked (address or radius-based)
- Small participant pool (neighbors, local residents)
- Problems are tangible, visible, fixable
- Compost stays local — energy from failed local ideas feeds other local ideas
- This is where someone without social media but with real local knowledge thrives

Think of it as: **A single garden bed.**

---

### Layer 2 — GROVE (Community)
**Your school district. Your neighborhood association. Your local organizations.**

Examples:
- "The PTA is wasting money on candy and cookies — here's what they should have done"
- "The community pool needs repairs and the HOA is misallocating funds"
- "The local food bank could serve 3x more people if they restructured distribution"

Properties:
- Community-scoped (school, organization, district)
- Medium participant pool (parents, members, residents)
- Problems involve group decisions and shared resources
- Rhetoric is common here (advertising, persuasion, tradition) — the lens works hard
- This is where institutional bullshit gets identified and consumed

Think of it as: **A cluster of trees sharing root systems.**

---

### Layer 3 — FOREST (City / Region)
**Your city. Your metro area. Your state-level concerns.**

Examples:
- "Traffic bottleneck at Lamar and 5th — remove this turn, adjust that light"
- "Austin's water infrastructure is aging and nobody's talking about it"
- "The zoning laws in District 7 are designed to benefit developers, not residents"

Properties:
- City/region-scoped
- Large participant pool
- Problems involve policy, infrastructure, systems
- This is where the system starts competing with local media and government discourse
- Plants here need stronger roots (more verification, more contribution history) to survive

Think of it as: **A forest ecosystem with canopy, understory, and floor.**

---

### Layer 4 — BIOSPHERE (National / Global)
**The country. The world. Systemic human problems.**

Examples:
- "The prison system creates laws that benefit lawmakers — here's the loop"
- "People can't earn enough for basic dopamine needs — the employment structure is broken"
- "Energy policy is captured by rhetoric from both sides — here's the structural reality"

Properties:
- No geographic limit
- Massive participant pool
- Problems are systemic, multi-generational, deeply entrenched
- This is where memetics (generational bullshit) gets identified and composted
- This is where the strongest logic and the strongest rhetoric collide
- The Alpha-Omega lens and Three Monkeys Lens work hardest here

Think of it as: **The entire biosphere — atmosphere, oceans, continents, all interconnected.**

## CROSS-BIOME MECHANICS

### Energy Flows Between Biomes

A plant in a Plot (hyperlocal) can grow into a Grove issue, then a Forest issue, then a Biosphere issue. Example:

1. **Plot:** "The power lines on my street are ugly and expensive"
2. **Grove:** "The whole neighborhood is overpaying for above-ground power"
3. **Forest:** "Austin's power grid design is 40 years behind"
4. **Biosphere:** "The global energy infrastructure model is fundamentally broken"

Each escalation requires MORE energy (bigger problem = bigger cost to plant) but the potential growth is proportionally larger.

### Compost Flows DOWN

When a Biosphere-level post gets consumed, the compost energy flows downward:
- Global idea fails → national conversations benefit
- National idea fails → city-level thinkers absorb it
- City idea fails → community-level benefits
- Community idea fails → local neighbors benefit

Like nutrients flowing from the canopy to the forest floor through rain and decomposition.

### Roots Can Reach UP

A Plot-level plant with enough energy and contribution can extend roots into Grove, Forest, or Biosphere:
- "My street's power problem" → connects to "Austin's grid problem" → connects to "global energy"
- The SAME plant, growing through biome layers
- This is how local knowledge becomes systemic insight

## BIOME CREATION

### System-Default Biomes
These exist from day one:
- Plot (GPS/address-based, auto-generated per area)
- Grove (community-defined, created by users when a group forms)
- Forest (city/region-defined, auto-generated per metro area)
- Biosphere (global, always exists)

### User-Created Biomes
Like subreddits — users can create topic-specific biomes within any layer:
- Plot: "5th Street Drainage" (hyperlocal, specific problem)
- Grove: "Eastside PTA" (community, specific organization)
- Forest: "Austin Infrastructure" (city, broad topic)
- Biosphere: "Global Energy Systems" (world, systemic)

Creation costs energy. Bigger biome scope = higher cost.

## THE STEWARD MIRROR

The Alpha-Omega lens applied TO the steward. What it shows:

### Mirror Questions (Run Continuously)

1. **Structure or Ideology?**
   - Is the steward's action based on the system's structural rules, or on personal belief/preference?
   - If ideology → mirror flags it

2. **Signal or Noise?**
   - Is the steward protecting signal or creating noise?
   - Every steward action must increase signal-to-noise ratio
   - If net noise increases → mirror flags it

3. **Falsifiable?**
   - Can the steward's decision be tested and proven wrong?
   - "I removed this because it violated rule X" is falsifiable
   - "I removed this because it felt wrong" is NOT
   - If unfalsifiable → mirror flags it

4. **Reversible?**
   - Would the system function the same without this intervention?
   - If yes → the intervention was unnecessary (noise)
   - If no → the intervention was structural (signal)

5. **Self-Serving?**
   - Does the steward's action benefit the steward's own plants/energy?
   - If yes → mirror flags it as conflict of interest

6. **Transparent?**
   - Can every user see what the steward did and why?
   - All steward actions are public
   - The mirror is visible to everyone — the steward operates in glass

### Mirror Output

The mirror doesn't STOP the steward. It EXPOSES the steward.

Just like any post, the steward's actions can be:
- Consumed (if someone proves the action was ideological, not structural)
- Composted (if the action fails, its energy returns to the system)
- Grown (if the action genuinely protected signal)

The steward is subject to the same rules as everyone else. The lens doesn't make exceptions.
